Steps

  1. Decide on the behavior that you want to increase or decrease.
    • For example, "Walk longer distances" or "Decrease cigarette smoking"
  2. Decide on the units to measure your behavior.
    • For example, "steps," "cigarettes," "minutes," or "oz"
  3. Note whether you want the behavior to increase or decrease
  4. Write a strong reason for why you want to better this behavior.
    • This will stay posted at the top of the page to remind you if things get tough
  5. Begin tracking your behavior at baseline
    • Don't make any changes in the behavior, just do what you normally would do.
  6. Go to the program on your account page and submit an entry for your daily baseline total.
    • Try to track your baseline for at least 3 days straight to get an accurate average
  7. Your first intervention goal will be automatically generated based on your baseline average
    • After you've met your goal, click "new goal" to challenge yourself even further!
  8. When you're ready, start tracking your behavior during intervention
    • Try your hardest to meet your goal every day, and use the motivational tools for inspiration!
  9. Track your progress on your account page and enjoy being your better self!
